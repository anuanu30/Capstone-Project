# Business Memo

## Subject: Customer Churn Risk Findings

The exploratory analysis identified several behavioral patterns associated with customer churn.

Customers with low web engagement, fewer sessions, and reduced ordering activity showed significantly higher churn likelihood. Increased support-ticket activity was also associated with elevated churn risk, suggesting customer dissatisfaction or unresolved service issues.

Customers with high abandoned-cart behavior may be facing checkout friction or pricing concerns. Campaign exposure analysis suggests that retention interventions may positively influence customer engagement.

Before launching any retention campaign, the company should further investigate:

- customer-service resolution quality,
- checkout and cart abandonment issues,
- declining user engagement patterns,
- targeting effectiveness of retention campaigns,
- high-risk customer segments based on behavioral activity.

These findings should guide future churn-prediction modeling and retention strategy design.
